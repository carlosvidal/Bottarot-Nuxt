When the Major Arcana number 7, The Chariot, appears in a reading about a **career change**, it brings a powerful message about direction, control, and the necessary drive to move forward. This card, a symbol of iron will and victory through conscious effort, suggests the querent is at a crucial moment where their decisions and determination will directly shape the **probable outcome** of their professional transition. It is not a card of chance, but of deliberate steering toward a chosen destination.

## Meaning of The Chariot in a Career Change

The Chariot in the context of a **career change** represents the energy of directed movement and mastery over seemingly opposing forces. It does not speak of a random change, but of one that is sought, planned, and executed with determination. The card suggests the querent has, or must take, the reins of their career. The **probable outcome** sketched by this card is intimately linked to the person's ability to stay focused, overcome internal obstacles (like doubt or fear) and external ones (like competition or complex processes), and advance confidently toward their goal.

The energy of The Chariot indicates the career change will not be a mere leap into the void, but a journey. The charioteer is not adrift; they have a clear destination and the means to get there. In practical terms, this can translate to selection processes where the candidate stands out for their clarity of ideas, the ability to negotiate favorable conditions for a new job, or success in launching their own project or business. The tarot's message here is one of personal agency: the outcome is in your hands.

However, The Chariot also warns of the need for balance. The charioteer must guide the black and white sphinxes (or horses), which pull in symbolically opposite directions. In a **career change**, this can represent the conflict between the security of the known and the desire for the new, between reason and passion, or between different job offers or career paths. The most favorable **probable outcome** arises when the querent manages to harmonize these tensions, using both forces (caution and drive, for example) to propel their career forward, rather than letting them pull it apart.

## Upright Interpretation

*   **Victory through directed effort:** The **probable outcome** points to a successful resolution of the change process, but this success is presented as a conquest. It's not luck; it's the fruit of a well-prepared application, an interview mastered with confidence, or an effective job search strategy.
*   **Taking control and direction:** The card suggests the querent is in a position to make important decisions and assume leadership of their own career. The outcome will be marked by this proactive attitude, potentially culminating in a role with greater autonomy, responsibility, or room to maneuver.
*   **Advancement and rapid movement:** Once a decision is made and action is taken, progress can be notable and relatively fast. The Chariot is a card of journeying, so the **probable outcome** may include a geographical change, frequent travel, or integration into a dynamic and competitive environment.
*   **Need for focus and determination:** The message underscores that distraction is the greatest enemy. To materialize the most positive outcome, the querent must maintain an unbreakable will and a clear vision of their professional goal, without being distracted by noise or minor setbacks.

## Reversed Interpretation

*   **Lack of direction or control:** The **probable outcome** may be clouded by indecision, lack of a clear plan, or a feeling of being adrift in the job search. It may indicate applying for many positions without a strategy, which dilutes energy and reduces chances.
*   **Unresolved conflicting forces:** Internal or external tensions (e.g., fear of change vs. dissatisfaction) can paralyze or cause setbacks. The outcome could be stagnation, rejections due to not conveying confidence, or accepting a change that doesn't resolve the original conflicts.
*   **Counterproductive aggression or impulsiveness:** The will of The Chariot, when reversed, can turn into stubbornness or aggression. A negative probable outcome could arise from burning bridges, excessive pressure in a negotiation, or making an impulsive decision without weighing the long-term consequences.
*   **Warning about obstacles:** The reversed card can point to real barriers (fierce competition, closed markets) that require a reevaluation of strategy, not just more brute force. Insisting on the same path without adapting can lead to frustration.

## Practical Advice

To align with the constructive energy of The Chariot and influence the **probable outcome** of your **career change**, action is key. Don't wait for opportunities to come; build them. Define your professional destination with the greatest possible clarity: what position, in what sector, under what conditions? Write your goal as if it were the final point on a map.

Then, design your route plan. The charioteer needs a vehicle and a path. Your vehicle is your skills, your CV, and your network. Make sure they are in optimal condition. Your path is the strategy: which companies to apply to, how to prepare for each interview, what skills to develop. Identify the "two forces" pulling you (e.g., "need for financial stability" vs. "desire for a creative job") and actively seek a role or path that can satisfy both, rather than choosing only one.

Finally, advance with determination, but with your eyes wide open. Review your progress, adjust your course if necessary, but don't abandon the wheel. Every conscious action brings you closer to the outcome The Chariot promises: a personal and professional victory you have forged with your own hands.

## Final Reflection

The Chariot, in its essence, reminds us that we are the drivers of our own professional journey. Its appearance at a time of **career change** is a powerful invitation to leave the role of passenger or spectator and take control of the voyage. The **probable outcome** it foresees is not a fixed destination, but the direct consequence of the clarity, courage, and applied will we decide to exercise. In the silent dialogue between desire and action, this card urges us to let action have the leading voice.

> "It is not the wind, but how you adjust the sails that determines your course. Take the reins of your Chariot and steer your will toward the horizon you have chosen."
