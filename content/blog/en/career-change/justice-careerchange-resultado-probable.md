When the Justice card appears in a reading about a **career change**, its presence is profoundly significant. It does not announce a casual twist of fate, but a moment of karmic evaluation and balance in the professional sphere. The energy of this card suggests that past decisions and actions are about to bear fruit, and the likely outcome will be intimately linked to the principles of fairness, truth, and responsibility.

## Meaning of Justice in a Career Change

Justice, as Major Arcana number 11, transcends the mere legal concept to speak to us of a universal law of cause and effect applied to our professional life. In the context of a **career change**, its appearance indicates that the ongoing process is not random. The card suggests you are at a point where your skills, your work ethic, and the decisions you have made (accepting a project, leaving another, your way of relating) are being weighed on the cosmic scales. The **likely outcome** that is glimpsed is one where balance will be restored.

This energy can manifest in several ways. It may indicate that you will receive an offer that accurately reflects your worth and experience, neither inflated nor underestimated. It can also signal that a selection process or salary negotiation will unfold with notable transparency, where all cards will be on the table. Justice does not promise easy promotions or blind strokes of luck; its message is deeper: the professional outcome will be a direct and proportional consequence of your preparation, your integrity, and your willingness to take responsibility.

Therefore, the focus on the **likely outcome** with Justice invites us to honest introspection. Have you been fair to yourself, properly valuing your talent? Have you acted with integrity in your current position? The answers to these questions are the key to interpreting the trend the card reveals. The change on the horizon has the potential to correct imbalances, whether by recognizing an unvalued effort or aligning your career with your true purpose.

## Upright Interpretation

When Justice presents itself in an upright position, its energy flows with clarity and fullness. In a **career change**, this translates to:

*   **Result of balanced negotiations:** The most likely scenario is that you achieve a work agreement (contract, conditions, salary) that is perceived as fair by all parties involved. The scales tip towards a satisfying equilibrium.
*   **Recognition of merit:** The card suggests a high probability that your experience, studies, and competencies will be evaluated objectively and lead to a favorable conclusion. It is the time to reap what you have sown with effort.
*   **Decisions based on reason and ethics:** Both your choices and those of potential employers will be guided by clear and transparent criteria, not by impulses or favoritism. Logic and equity prevail.
*   **Resolution of pending situations:** It may indicate the closing of a professional stage in a formal and correct manner, settling pending matters and leaving accounts clear, which opens the door to a new beginning without burdens.

## Reversed Interpretation

Justice reversed modulates its message, signaling that the principle of balance exists but encounters obstructions or distortions in the context of the **career change**.

*   **Imbalance in conditions:** The likely outcome may include offers that do not reflect your real value, contracts with unfair clauses, or work environments where bias is present. The scales are unbalanced.
*   **Lack of objectivity:** It may suggest that in the selection process or in your own evaluation, prejudices, incomplete information, or misunderstandings will take precedence. It is crucial to seek clarity and document everything.
*   **Avoiding responsibilities:** The reversed energy sometimes warns of the temptation to take unethical shortcuts or not assume the consequences of past professional decisions, which could complicate the outcome.
*   **Delays in legal or administrative processes:** If there are contractual or legal aspects involved in the change, they could experience delays or bureaucratic complications that require patience and meticulous review.

## Practical Advice

To align yourself with the constructive energy of Justice and influence the **likely outcome** of your **career change**, action is key. Passivity is not compatible with this arcana.

*   **Document and evaluate honestly:** Make an objective inventory of your achievements, skills, and salary expectations. Be fair to yourself, neither underestimating nor overselling your talent. Internal clarity attracts external clarity.
*   **Seek equity in negotiation:** When presenting your conditions, appeal to logic, market standards, and your tangible contribution. Justice responds to well-founded arguments, not emotional demands.
*   **Read the fine print:** Faced with any offer, take time to understand every detail of the contract. Ask about what you don't understand. The energy of this card rewards diligence and attention to formal details.
*   **Reflect on your work karma:** Consider if there are pending situations or damaged professional relationships that need to be healed or closed with integrity. Acting with rectitude now lays the foundation for future balance.

## Final Reflection

Justice, in the journey of a professional change, is not a distant judge who passes sentence, but a mirror that reflects the natural consequence of our own choices. Its appearance reminds us that in the world of work, as in life, there is a law of reciprocity. The **likely outcome** it points to is an invitation to consciously participate in the creation of our professional destiny, armed with truth, responsibility, and the courage to seek what is equitable, both for ourselves and for others. The path to satisfying work often begins with the commitment to be fair to our own worth.

> "True justice in your career is not what you receive, but the balance you create between what you give and what you honor in yourself."
