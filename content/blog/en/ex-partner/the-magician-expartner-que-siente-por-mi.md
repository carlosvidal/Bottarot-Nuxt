When the **The Magician** card appears in a reading about an ex-partner, the universe invites us to look beyond the surface emotions. This card, number one of the Major Arcana, does not speak of passive emotions, but of an active energy, of will, and of a potential seeking to be channeled. In the context of 'what do they feel for me,' **The Magician tarot ex partner** immerses us in a symbolism of personal power, conscious intention, and the ability to shape reality. It is not a card of pure nostalgia, but of action and potential manifestation.

## Meaning of The Magician for an Ex-Partner

In the emotional landscape of a past relationship, **The Magician** represents an energy of mental clarity and purpose. It does not suggest a confused or nebulous feeling, but a fairly defined perception of what you were and, crucially, of what *could be*. The person in question does not see you only as a memory, but as an element on their board of possibilities. The energy of this card indicates that your ex-partner may feel that the connection you shared had (or still has) a unique potential, an emotional 'raw material' with which something new could be built.

The symbolism of **The Magician** points to the power of communication and skill. In terms of feelings, this can translate to your ex perceiving the relationship as a space where communication (for better or worse) was potent, where words and intentions carried weight. It is not an energy of victimhood or pure overflowing emotion; it is an energy of mind over heart, or at least, of a mind trying to direct the heart. When we ask **'The Magician what do they feel for me'**, the answer usually orbits around respect for your individuality and your capabilities, seeing you as someone with whom they could 'co-create' a reality.

Finally, the tarot's message through **The Magician** in this context is an invitation to observe intention. The feelings your ex-partner projects might be tinged with a will to influence, to reconnect with a purpose, or to heal from a place of empowerment. It is not a passive energy of longing; it is active. They may feel that between you there are unused tools, pending conversations that could change things, or simply, the perception that what you lived through left you both more 'powerful' in a sense. The focus is on potential and on the personal agency of both.

## Upright Interpretation

*   **Mental Clarity and Focus:** Your ex-partner probably remembers and thinks of you with notable clarity. They are not just diffuse emotions; there is a conscious evaluation of what you were, your qualities, and the role you played in their life. Their feeling is mixed with recognition of your personal power.
*   **Perception of Potential and Opportunity:** The energy of **The Magician** suggests they may feel that the story between you is not completely closed from a growth perspective. They see you as part of a potential, as a card that could still be played in the game of their life, whether for reconnection, closure, or mutual learning.
*   **Willingness for Communication or Action:** This is not a passive feeling. **The Magician** indicates intention. They might be considering, or even planning, to take a step to communicate, clarify something, or test if that 'potential' they perceive can manifest. They feel they have the 'tools' (courage, words, memories) to try.
*   **Respect for Your Autonomy:** Unlike more fused cards, **The Magician** recognizes individuality. Their feeling likely includes respect for your path and your power. They do not see you as someone who 'needs' to be rescued, but as an equal to interact with.

## Reversed Interpretation

*   **Manipulation or Hidden Intentions:** The reversal of **The Magician** can indicate that your ex's feelings or intentions are not transparent. There could be a desire for reconnection based on self-interest, on filling a void, or on exerting an influence over you that is not entirely clean. Beware of power games.
*   **Wasted or Blocked Potential:** Instead of seeing opportunity, they may feel frustration or powerlessness regarding what could not be built. Their feelings might be stuck in the 'what if...', but without the will or skill to transform that energy into something healthy.
*   **Faulty or Deceptive Communication:** If there is an intention for contact, it could be tainted. Empty promises, words that don't align with actions, or communication that seeks to confuse rather than clarify. The energy suggests that not everything is as it seems in their emotional discourse.
*   **Lack of Their Own Personal Power:** Sometimes, **The Magician** reversed reflects that your ex sees you as a source of power or validation that they have lost. The 'feeling' might be more a projection of their own lack than a genuine, balanced affection for you.

## Practical Advice

When **The Magician** appears in a reading about **tarot ex partner**, the advice most aligned with its energy is to reclaim your own power. This card reminds you that you are the main person in your life. Regardless of what your ex-partner feels or thinks, you have control over your tools: your will, your word, and your focus.

1.  **Reflect on Your Own Power:** Take time to evaluate what you learned from that relationship and how it strengthened you. **The Magician** urges you to recognize your own skills and emotional resources.
2.  **Be Clear in Your Boundaries:** If communication arises, channel the energy of **The Magician upright**. Be intentional with your words, clear in your boundaries, and aware of what you wish to manifest (or not) from now on.
3.  **Observe, Don't Project:** Do not assume that the potential they may see is a shared truth. Distinguish between a fantasy of reconnection and a mutual, healthy desire based on the present.
4.  **Focus Your Energy on Your Present:** Use the manifestation energy of **The Magician** for your own benefit. What do you want to create in your love and personal life now? Focus your tools on building your future, not just analyzing the past.

## Final Reflection

The **The Magician** card in the context of an ex-partner acts as a mirror that reflects both the other person's intention and, most importantly, your own capacity for agency. It reminds us that relationships, even those that end, leave in our hands tools for self-knowledge and personal power. More than obsessing over deciphering another's feelings, the invitation is to ask ourselves: how do I use the tools I have now to heal, grow, and manifest the love life I deserve?

> The true magic does not lie in guessing another's feelings, but in recognizing one's own power to transform our own story.
